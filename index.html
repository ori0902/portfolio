Two-Level Simulation Model for Compressible Turbulent Scalar Mixing

UTC Chattanooga â€¢ 2022

Assessed Two-Level Simulation (TLS) modeling assumptions for compressible scalar mixing at convective Mach number 0.7 and Reynolds number 640 using Direct Numerical Simulation (DNS) with fully compressible solver.

Applied a scale-separation methodology that decomposes scalar fields into large- and small-scale components, solving simplified small-scale equations on embedded 1D lines without traditional LES filtering.

Shown that the TLS model captures key subgrid physics, including counter-gradient transport, anisotropy, and backscatter, with results presented at the 17th International Workshop on Physics of Compressible Turbulent Mixing.

UTC Chattanooga â€¢ 2022

Extended Two-Level Simulation framework to turbulent premixed methane-air flames using 4-step, 8-species mechanism on 256Â³ grid, validating modeling assumptions for finite-rate chemistry in thin reaction zone regime.

Non-Equilibrium Plasma-Assisted Combustion Kinetics

Auburn University â€¢ 2019-2021

Built lab infrastructure and designed/operated purpose-built plasma-coupled flow reactor (PFR) to investigate non-equilibrium plasma effects on iso-octane pyrolysis and oxidation kinetics across 300-1200K temperature range.

Conducted isothermal flow-reactor experiments with nanosecond pulsed plasma discharge, analyzing product species via GC-MS and supporting the work with a 0D plasma kinetic solver in Fortran coupled to CHEMKIN.

Showed that non-equilibrium plasma markedly accelerates low-temperature fuel decomposition, informing the design of low-temperature plasma ignition concepts for advanced engines.
